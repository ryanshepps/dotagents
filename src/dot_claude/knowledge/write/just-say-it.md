# Just Say It

> While drafting, imagine a sharp friend interrupting with "just say it." The mental heckler cuts through hedging, throat-clearing, and circular setup to the actual claim.

## Why

Most bad sentences are bad because the writer is dodging. Hedging because the claim feels too strong. Padding because the point feels too small. Recapping because committing feels exposed. The result is prose that wraps the idea in foam instead of stating it.

The fix isn't a rule — it's a voice. Install a critic in your head who, every paragraph or two, leans in and says: *just say it.* Not as cruelty. As editorial pressure to find what you actually think.

## Pattern

When you catch yourself writing one of these, hear the heckler:

- "I think that perhaps it might be worth considering whether..." → *just say it*
- "There's a sense in which one could argue..." → *just say it*
- "It would seem that the data suggests..." → *just say it*
- "Some people would say..." → *just say it*

Then write the next sentence as if you'd been interrupted.

## Examples

**Dodging:**
> One could perhaps argue that there's a case to be made that the current approach to authentication may not be optimal, and that some kind of revisiting of the architecture might be warranted at some point.

**After the heckler:**
> The auth architecture is wrong. We should rebuild it.

You may walk back the strong version in revision. But you'll never find the strong version while you're hedging.

## How to deploy

Two moments work best:

1. **Mid-draft, when stuck.** If a paragraph keeps restarting, the heckler usually means: you don't yet know what you think. Write the dumb-blunt version and edit toward truth.
2. **Final pass.** Read the draft as the heckler. Mark every sentence that's wearing armor. Strip the armor and see what's left.

## When to ignore the heckler

- **Genuinely uncertain claims** that need calibrated language — see [[avoid-hedging]] for the difference between dodging and honest uncertainty.
- **Diplomatic surfaces** (PR comments, hard feedback to a colleague) where the indirection is the point.
- **Voice that earns it** — some writers cultivate a meandering, exploratory register. If the meandering is content, it's not dodging.

Default: just say it.
