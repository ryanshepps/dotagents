# Platform-Native Structure

> Each platform has its own form. X rewards brevity and wit; LinkedIn rewards white space and business framing; threads run serial. Don't cross-post identically — rewrite for the surface.

## Why

Cross-posting the same text everywhere is the social-media equivalent of using the same email template for your CEO, your spouse, and your customer. The audience differs, the medium differs, the conventions differ. A post optimized for one platform will underperform on the others — sometimes badly enough to look amateur.

The fix isn't to write five posts from scratch. It's to write one idea, then *re-form* it for each surface using that platform's conventions.

## Conventions (May 2026)

### X / Twitter

- **Tone:** brief, sharp, opinionated. Wit lands.
- **Length:** single posts under 280 chars or full long-form (Premium). Avoid mid-length.
- **Threads:** sequential atomic essays. Strong hook on post 1 (see [[hook-stops-the-scroll]]); each subsequent post stands alone.
- **Formatting:** line breaks aggressive. No hashtags. Links cripple reach (see [[zero-click-content]]).

### LinkedIn

- **Tone:** professional but personal. First-person stories about work. "Vulnerability + lesson" is the dominant form.
- **Length:** 1,200-1,500 chars sweet spot. The "see more" cut at ~3 lines is the second hook — earn the expand.
- **Formatting:** heavy white space. Single-sentence paragraphs. Sometimes emoji bullets (·, →, ✅) for scannability.
- **CTA:** end with a question that prompts a comment. Comments are the engagement currency.

### Threads / Bluesky

- **Tone:** more casual than LinkedIn, less performative than X.
- **Length:** flexible, leans short.
- **Formatting:** similar to X, slightly more tolerant of dense paragraphs.

### Substack Notes

- **Tone:** essayistic fragments. Lower hook pressure than X — the audience opted in.
- **Length:** medium. Often a paragraph of thinking, not a punchline.

## Pattern

For one core idea, draft 3 versions:

1. **X version:** sharpest single-line claim + one supporting beat.
2. **LinkedIn version:** opening scene → lesson → ask. 4-6 short paragraphs.
3. **Substack note version:** the fuller thought, no hook pressure.

This is 15 extra minutes per idea. The performance delta justifies it.

## Anti-pattern

Auto-syndication tools (Buffer, Hypefury) that push the same text to all platforms. Convenient; consistently underperforms manual rewrites. Use them only when reach isn't the goal (e.g., archival cross-posting for your own record).

## In critique

Open the post on each platform it will run on. Anything that looks "off" — too long, too short, wrong tone, broken formatting — needs a platform-specific rewrite, not a global edit.
