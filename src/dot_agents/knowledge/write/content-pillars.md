# Content Pillars

> Pick 3-5 recurring topics you post about. Brand is a pattern in the feed. Random posting reads as noise; pillared posting reads as a point of view.

## Why

A new follower decides whether to stay based on the *next 3 posts* they see from you, not the one they just liked. If those posts are about wildly different things, they don't form a pattern — they form a question ("what is this account about?"). Question mark loses to clarity. They unfollow.

Pillars are the answer to "what should I post about today?" and to "what is this person known for?" They're load-bearing for both the writer's decisions and the reader's expectations.

Justin Welsh's frame: 3-5 pillars, each ~20-25% of your posts. Anything outside the pillars is rare enough to feel deliberate, not random.

## Pattern

Pick pillars at the intersection of:

1. **What you know.** Domains where you have specific opinions and earned scars.
2. **What your target audience wants.** Topics they search, struggle with, debate.
3. **What you can sustain.** Pillars you'll still want to post about in 12 months.

Bad pillars: "leadership" (too vague), "life" (not a topic), "AI" (too crowded without an angle). Good pillars: "engineering hiring at <50-person startups," "rebuilding focus after burnout," "going from IC to first-time founder."

The narrower the pillar, the stronger the brand. Three sharp pillars beat seven blurry ones.

## How to use them

- **Before posting**, name which pillar this post lives in. If none, decide: skip, or earn a side-topic.
- **In a content calendar**, rotate pillars so no single one dominates a week.
- **In your bio**, name the pillars (or the through-line that unifies them). See [[profile-as-funnel]].

## When to break the pattern

- Major personal news, rare and brief.
- A genuine new interest you're committing to — but then it becomes a pillar.
- Reacting to a moment in your field (a launch, an incident). Frequency: weeks apart, not daily.

A feed that breaks pattern weekly has no pattern. A feed that breaks pattern quarterly has a strong one with personality.

## In critique

Read the last 10 posts. Can you name 3-5 themes? If not, the brand isn't compounding — every post starts from zero.
