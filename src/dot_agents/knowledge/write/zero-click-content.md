# Zero-Click Content

> Give the full payload inside the post. Don't tease and link out. Algorithms suppress off-platform links; readers reward in-feed value with engagement that compounds.

## Why

Coined by Amanda Natividad (SparkToro). The premise: social platforms are walled gardens that exist to keep users on the platform. They algorithmically suppress posts that try to redirect attention away (external links, "link in bio," YouTube embeds). They algorithmically reward posts that deliver value *in the feed* — long dwell time, replies, reshares.

The implication for personal-brand posting: if you write to drive clicks to your blog, the post underperforms, fewer people see it, fewer click. If you write to deliver the lesson in-post, more people see it, the brand compounds, and the readers who *do* want more come find you.

Tease-and-link is a 2010 playbook. Give-it-away is the 2020s playbook.

## Pattern

For any piece of content you want to amplify:

1. **Extract the core insight.** One sentence.
2. **Write a self-contained post that delivers it.** No "read more for the full breakdown."
3. **Optionally**: at the end, mention the longer piece exists ("Wrote 2,000 more words on this — link in profile if you want it"). This is a signpost, not the point.

The post must stand alone. Someone who reads only the post should get the value. The link is for the 5% who want the deep cut.

## Examples

**Tease (avoid):**
> 5 lessons from my $40k mistake. Full breakdown in the link below ↓

**Zero-click:**
> 5 lessons from my $40k mistake:
>
> 1. [actual lesson]
> 2. [actual lesson]
> ...
>
> (Wrote up the full story w/ numbers — link in bio if you want it.)

The second version performs 5-10x better in the feed *and* drives more clicks in absolute terms, because more people see it.

## Cross-platform notes

- **X:** outbound links cripple reach. In-thread value, link at the end.
- **LinkedIn:** links in the post body suppress; link in first comment is the workaround, but in-post value still dominates.
- **Threads / Bluesky:** less aggressive suppression but the cultural norm leans the same way — value in-feed.

## Anti-pattern

Holding back the "real" insight for the click. Readers feel the bait. The few who click resent it; the many who don't unfollow.
