# Active Voice

> Default to active voice — name the actor before the action. Passive only when the actor is unknown, irrelevant, or deliberately obscured.

## Why

Active sentences are shorter, clearer, and more accountable. The reader knows who did what. Passive constructions hide the actor, add a helping verb, and force the reader to invert the sentence to recover meaning.

## Pattern

- **Active:** `<subject> <verb> <object>` — "Sarah shipped the migration."
- **Passive:** `<object> was <verbed> by <subject>` — "The migration was shipped by Sarah."

The passive almost always adds a word or two and pushes the actor to the end (or omits them entirely).

## When passive is right

Use passive deliberately, not by default:

1. **Actor unknown** — "The server was breached overnight." (You don't yet know by whom.)
2. **Actor irrelevant** — "The package was delivered." (Carrier doesn't matter to the point.)
3. **Object is the topic** — "The bill was vetoed by the governor." (When the bill, not the governor, is what the paragraph is about.)
4. **Diplomatic distance** — "Mistakes were made." (When you're choosing not to name the actor.)

If none of these apply, switch to active.

## How to spot passive

Search for these patterns in drafts:
- `was`, `were`, `is`, `are`, `been`, `being` followed by a past participle (`shipped`, `created`, `decided`)
- `by <noun>` near the end of a clause

Not every `was`/`were` is passive — `was running` is active progressive. Look for past-participle + `by` together.

## Examples

| Passive | Active |
|---------|--------|
| The decision was made by the team. | The team decided. |
| Tests are run by CI on every push. | CI runs tests on every push. |
| It is recommended that you upgrade. | We recommend you upgrade. |
| The bug was caused by a race condition. | A race condition caused the bug. |

## In tone-checking

When reviewing someone else's text, flag passive constructions and propose the active rewrite. Don't flag justified passive uses — name *why* the active version is stronger when you do flag.
