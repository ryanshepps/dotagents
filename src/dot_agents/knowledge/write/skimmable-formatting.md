# Skimmable Formatting

> Social posts get skimmed on mobile. Short paragraphs, line breaks between thoughts, no walls of text. Readability on a phone screen is the constraint.

## Why

The reader is on a phone, in a feed, between two other things. A wall of text reads as work. They scroll. Same words, broken into 1-2 line paragraphs with white space between, reads as easy — they stay.

Ship 30's framing: **skimmability is readability.** A post that's technically denser but visually airy gets read; a tight paragraph of the same content gets skipped.

## Pattern

- **1-3 lines per paragraph.** Anything longer becomes a wall on mobile.
- **Line break between every idea-beat.** If a sentence introduces a turn, give it air.
- **No multi-sentence paragraphs without a reason.** Reasons: a tight one-two punch, a list that flows as prose. Default is split.
- **Front-load the line.** First 2-3 words of each line do the work — the eye lands there first.

## Example

**Wall (avoid):**
> I used to write long posts thinking the depth would impress people but what I learned after 2 years is that depth doesn't matter if nobody reads to it, the best posts I've written look almost too short on the page and the reader gets to the end without realizing they read anything.

**Skimmable:**
> I used to write long posts thinking depth would impress people.
>
> Two years in, I learned depth doesn't matter if nobody reads to it.
>
> The best posts I've written look almost too short on the page.
>
> Reader gets to the end without realizing they read anything.

Same content. The second one gets finished.

## Platform tuning

- **X:** line breaks cost no characters — use them aggressively.
- **LinkedIn:** the "see more" cut-off lives ~3 lines deep. First three lines must earn the expand. Heavy white space below.
- **Threads / Bluesky:** similar to X, slightly more tolerant of dense paragraphs.

See [[platform-native-structure]] for surface-specific tuning.

## In critique

Open the draft on a phone (or narrow the browser). Anywhere your eye refuses to start a paragraph, the author needs a break. Mark those spots — don't rewrite, just point at the wall.
