# Mental Model

> Users interact based on what they think the system does — friction spikes when the interface violates their model.

## Key Takeaways

- Users bring models from prior software. Match those models where possible; when you cannot, invest in teaching the new one explicitly.
- Cost of mismatch is asymmetric. A user acting on a wrong model may not notice until they lose work or trust.
- Probe mental models in research with "what do you think happens when you click this?" — behavior follows belief, not reality.
