# Law of Similarity

> Elements that look alike read as related — use shared styling to communicate grouping and role.

## Key Takeaways

- Shared color, shape, size, or typography signals "same kind of thing." Links should look like links; errors like errors.
- Inverse is equally true: similarity across unrelated items implies a relationship users will look for and fail to find.
- Use similarity to reinforce a design system — buttons, inputs, and cards should be instantly re-recognizable across screens.
