# Miller's Law

> Working memory holds about 7±2 items — design around this limit, not against it.

## Key Takeaways

- The 7±2 figure is a working-memory heuristic, not a hard UI cap. The takeaway is: small groups process fast, large ones do not.
- Chunking defeats the limit. Break long sequences (phone numbers, steps, menus) into 3–5 digestible groups.
- Do not cite "7 items max" as a law of menu length. Context matters — a scannable list of 30 is fine; a branching decision of 30 is not.
