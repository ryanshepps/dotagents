# Doherty Threshold

> Keep system response under 400ms — below that threshold, users stay in flow and productivity climbs sharply.

## Key Takeaways

- Over ~400ms, users notice the wait, context-switch, and lose momentum. Under it, interaction feels instantaneous and engagement compounds.
- When you cannot hit 400ms, fake it: optimistic UI, skeletons, progress feedback, or prefetching. Perceived performance is the goal.
- Prioritize latency on hot paths (input, navigation, search) — background work tolerates more.
