# Fitts's Law

> Time to click a target is a function of its distance and size — make important targets large and close.

## Key Takeaways

- Large, nearby targets are fast to acquire. Small, distant targets cost time and invite errors. Primary CTAs earn size and prominence; secondary ones shrink.
- Screen edges and corners are effectively infinite targets (pointer stops there) — exploit for persistent UI like menu bars and dock icons.
- On touch, 44×44pt (iOS) or 48×48dp (Android) is the minimum comfortable hit target. Space tappable items to avoid mis-taps.
