# Hick's Law

> Decision time grows with the number and complexity of options — fewer, simpler choices mean faster action.

## Key Takeaways

- Every extra option adds cognitive overhead. Trim, group, or hide options until only the relevant ones are visible.
- Progressive disclosure (show more on demand) keeps the initial choice small without sacrificing capability.
- For expert users, shortcuts and search bypass the menu entirely — that is Hick's Law dodged, not violated.
